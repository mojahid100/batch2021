GEP all Internal Medicine treatments Flashcards

Constipation & Diarrhea

What are the treatments for constipation (general)?
Treat cause; high-fiber diet; increase fluids; laxatives: bulk-forming (bran, methylcellulose), osmotic (lactulose, Mg sulfate), stimulant (phenolphthalein), suppository (glycerin), enema (hypertonic phosphate).
What are the treatments for slow-transit constipation?
Laxatives (bulk, osmotic, stimulant); prokinetics (tegaserod, prucalopride); secretagogues (lubiprostone, linaclotide, plecanatide); colectomy with ileorectostomy if medical treatment fails (2-3 months) or megacolon/megarectum present.
What are the treatments for pelvic floor dysfunction?
Biofeedback training to correct dyssynergic defecation and improve rectal sensory perception.
What are the treatments for rectal outlet obstruction?
Surgery for rectocele, rectal prolapse, rectal intussusception, hemorrhoids, or anal fissure.
What are the treatments for refractory constipation?
Sacral nerve stimulation for refractory hypomotility or inability to evacuate.
What are the treatments for diarrhea?
Treat cause; rehydration: oral (Na, K, Cl, HCO3, glucose) or IV (with vomiting); drugs: absorbents (kaopectate), antisecretory (bismuth subsalicylate), opiates (loperamide, diphenoxylate+atropine, avoid in fever/toxemia), anticholinergics (for IBS), antimicrobials (cipro, trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole, nitazoxanide, rifaximin), antiparasitics.

Lower & Upper GI Bleeding

What are the initial steps for stabilizing a patient with UGI bleeding?
Assess hemodynamic stability, provide supplemental oxygen (goal ≥94% saturation for non-COPD patients), monitor airway, vital signs, cardiac rhythm, urine output, and nasogastric output if tube in place.
What is the protocol for intravenous access in UGI bleeding?
Start two 18-gauge or larger IV lines, keep patient NPO, send blood for CBC, PT/INR, creatinine, liver enzymes, cross-matching. For unstable patients: give 0.9% saline or lactated Ringer’s, cross-match 2–4 units PRBCs. For stable patients: delay aggressive fluid repletion until bleeding extent clarified.
How is fluid resuscitation managed in UGI bleeding?
Begin immediately with IV fluids in boluses (500–1000 mL) over 15–30 minutes using pressure bags, repeat until BP and tissue perfusion are acceptable.
What are the criteria for blood transfusion in UGI bleeding?
Transfuse if Hb <9 g/dL (high-risk patients, e.g., cardiovascular disease) or <7 g/dL (low-risk patients) with continued bleeding. Give plasma for coagulopathy or after 4 units RBCs, platelets for thrombocytopenia (<50,000) or dysfunction, Fresh Frozen Plasma if INR >1.8. Avoid over-transfusion in variceal bleeding.
What is the role of balloon tamponade in UGI bleeding?
Used as a temporizing measure for uncontrollable variceal hemorrhage with devices like Sengstaken-Blakemore or Minnesota tube. Requires tracheal intubation and proper placement to avoid esophageal rupture.
What medications are used for UGI bleeding before endoscopy?
IV PPI (80 mg bolus, then 40 mg every 12 hours until endoscopy). Erythromycin (250 mg IV over 20–30 min, monitor QTc, check drug interactions). For variceal bleeding: somatostatin or octreotide (50 mcg IV bolus, then 50 mcg/hour infusion). Antibiotics (e.g., ceftriaxone, levofloxacin) for cirrhosis.
How is variceal bleeding treated endoscopically?
Endoscopic band ligation; sclerotherapy if EBL not feasible. Additional intervention for Child B with active bleeding or Child C <14.
What are the portal decompressive procedures for uncontrolled variceal bleeding?
Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt (TIPS): stent creates portosystemic shunt. Emergency portosystemic shunt surgery (40–60% mortality rate).
How is re-bleeding prevented in UGI variceal bleeding?
Combine nonselective beta-blockers (propranolol, nadolol) and variceal band ligation (reduces rebleeding to 40% and 30%, respectively). TIPS as additional measure.
What is the initial treatment for acute colonic LGI bleeding?
Volume resuscitation plus blood transfusion.
What are the therapeutic uses of colonoscopy for LGI bleeding?
Epinephrine injection, electrocautery, endoscopic clips. If hemorrhage recurs, colonic resection is indicated.
How is mesenteric angiography used therapeutically for LGI bleeding?
Embolization of the culprit vessel to control bleeding.
What are the treatment options for diverticulitis-related LGI bleeding?
Medical therapy initially. Surgery indicated for no improvement, ≥2 attacks, complicated diverticulitis, or recurrent/persistent hemorrhage.
How are neoplasms causing LGI bleeding managed?
Proper evaluation, biopsy, staging for chemotherapy, radiotherapy, and/or oncosurgery.
What is the implied treatment for colitis-related LGI bleeding?
Medical management (e.g., anti-inflammatory or immunosuppressive therapy for ulcerative colitis/Crohn’s). For C. difficile colitis, antibiotics like metronidazole or vancomycin implied.
How is ischemic colitis typically managed?
Supportive care (e.g., hydration, monitoring) as most cases are mild and self-limited.
What treatments are available for angiodysplasia causing LGI bleeding?
Colonoscopic interventions, angiography and embolization, medication (unspecified), or occasionally surgery.
How are internal hemorrhoids treated based on degree?
1st/2nd degree: dietary fiber, stool softeners, sitz bath, rubber band ligation, infrared photocoagulation, sclerotherapy. 3rd degree: rubber band ligation, sclerotherapy, hemorrhoidectomy. 4th degree: operative hemorrhoidectomy.
What is the treatment for external hemorrhoids?
Conservative management (e.g., pain relief, sitz baths) as they are usually self-limited.
How is an anal fissure treated?
Conservative management with antibiotics, analgesics, stool softeners, anal sphincter relaxants, local dry dressing.
What is the treatment for anal fistula causing LGI bleeding?
Surgical management depending on type and site of fistula.

Malabsorption Syndrome

What are the general treatment approaches for malabsorption syndrome?
Treat the underlying cause, provide vitamin supplementation, nutrient supplementation (parenteral), and pancreatic enzyme supplementation.
What dietary modifications are recommended for specific malabsorption conditions?
Gluten-free diet for celiac disease. Lactose avoidance for lactose intolerance.
How is small bowel bacterial overgrowth treated in malabsorption syndrome?
Antibiotic therapy.
What is the dietary treatment for celiac disease?
Avoid wheat, rye, and barley. Allow rice, corn, and oats. Recommend lactose-free diet until symptoms improve due to secondary lactase deficiency.
What additional treatment may be used for resistant cases of celiac disease?
Steroids in resistant cases.
How is tropical sprue treated?
Broad-spectrum antibiotics, oral folate, and vitamin B12 injections.
What is the treatment for Whipple’s disease?
Prolonged course of broad-spectrum antibiotics (e.g., ceftriaxone and trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole for 1 year).

GIT Motility

What are the treatments for achalasia?
Pharmacologic: Nitrates, Ca++ blocker (nifedipine, muscle relaxant); Endoscopic: Pneumatic dilatation (balloon), botulinum toxin injection; Surgical: Heller myotomy + Nissen, Toupet, or Dor fundoplication, Peroral endoscopic myotomy (POEM)
What are the treatments for gastroparesis?
Small frequent meals, avoid indigestible fibers, replace some calories from solid to calorically dense liquids; Prokinetic treatment (high doses: dopredone, motelium, prempram); Enteral feeding in resistant cases: Jejunostomy tube
What are the treatments for Ogilvie’s syndrome?
Conservative management, colonic decompression tube, cecostomy, right colectomy

Inflammatory Bowel Disease

What are the treatments for ulcerative colitis (UC)?
Remission: Aminosalicylates (mesalamine, sulfasalazine), corticosteroids (prednisone), methotrexate, antibiotics (more in Crohn’s), immunomodulators (azathioprine, mercaptopurine), biological treatment (TNF inhibitors: infliximab, adalimumab; anti-α4β7 integrin: vedolizumab); Active proctitis: 1 gm mesalazine suppository ± oral 5-ASA (1 gm/6h); Acute left-sided or pancolitis (mild/moderate): Oral and topical 5-ASA ± prednisolone (40 mg daily, tapered 5 mg/week, stopped after 8 weeks), Ca and Vit D with glucocorticoids; Glucocorticoids not for maintenance; Immunosuppressive (thiopurine); Severe UC: IV fluids for dehydration, IV glucocorticoid bolus, topical and oral 5-ASA, biological treatment; Surgical: Proctocolectomy (implied for severe/refractory cases); Maintaining remission: Aminosalicylates (mesalamine, sulfasalazine), biological treatment for moderate/severe UC intolerant/non-responsive to immunosuppression
What are the treatments for Crohn’s disease (CD)?
Glucocorticoids: Prednisolone (40 mg daily, reducing 5 mg/week over 8 weeks), budesonide (first choice for ileal disease, 90% first-pass metabolism, low systemic toxicity); Calcium and vitamin D supplements; Anti-TNF agents (infliximab, adalimumab); Combination therapy (anti-TNF + azathioprine); Elemental diet, TPN; Surgical: Resection of affected segments (implied for complications)

Metabolic Liver Diseases

What are the treatments for non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH)?
Lifestyle interventions: Dietary changes, physical exercise to promote weight loss and improve insulin sensitivity (7-10% weight loss target)
What are the treatments for alcoholic fatty liver disease (AFLD)?
Cessation of alcohol, nutrition, corticosteroids, pentoxifylline, liver transplantation
What are the treatments for hereditary hemochromatosis?
Venesection of 500 mL twice weekly for up to 2 years, deferoxamine (iron chelating agent), liver transplantation
What are the treatments for Wilson’s disease?
Penicillamine, trientine (syprine), zinc acetate (galzin), liver transplantation
What are the treatments for Budd-Chiari syndrome?
Treat cause, TIPS, anticoagulation; Chronic/fulminant: Liver transplantation + lifelong anticoagulation

HCC

What are the treatments for hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)?
Surgical: Resection, liver transplantation; Ablative therapies: Radiofrequency, percutaneous ethanol injection (PEI), microwave ablation (MWA), cryoablation; Transcatheter arterial chemoembolization (TACE); Systemic chemotherapy; Palliative treatment

Ascites

What are the treatments for ascites?
Spironolactone + furosemide, paracentesis
What are the treatments for spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP)?
Cefotaxime, norfloxacin
