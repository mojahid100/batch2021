GEP all Internal Medicine treatments Flashcards

Constipation & Diarrhea

What are the treatments for constipation (general)?
Treat cause; high-fiber diet; increase fluids; laxatives: bulk-forming (bran, methylcellulose), osmotic (lactulose, Mg sulfate), stimulant (phenolphthalein), suppository (glycerin), enema (hypertonic phosphate).
What are the treatments for slow-transit constipation?
Laxatives (bulk, osmotic, stimulant); prokinetics (tegaserod, prucalopride); secretagogues (lubiprostone, linaclotide, plecanatide); colectomy with ileorectostomy if medical treatment fails (2-3 months) or megacolon/megarectum present.
What are the treatments for pelvic floor dysfunction?
Biofeedback training to correct dyssynergic defecation and improve rectal sensory perception.
What are the treatments for rectal outlet obstruction?
Surgery for rectocele, rectal prolapse, rectal intussusception, hemorrhoids, or anal fissure.
What are the treatments for refractory constipation?
Sacral nerve stimulation for refractory hypomotility or inability to evacuate.
What are the treatments for diarrhea?
Treat cause; rehydration: oral (Na, K, Cl, HCO3, glucose) or IV (with vomiting); drugs: absorbents (kaopectate), antisecretory (bismuth subsalicylate), opiates (loperamide, diphenoxylate+atropine, avoid in fever/toxemia), anticholinergics (for IBS), antimicrobials (cipro, trimethoprim/sulfamethoxazole, nitazoxanide, rifaximin), antiparasitics.

Lower & Upper GI Bleeding

What are the initial stabilization steps for Lower GI Bleeding?
Volume resuscitation, blood transfusion if hemoglobin <6 g/dL or hematocrit <30%.
What interventions are used in therapeutic colonoscopy for Lower GI Bleeding?
Epinephrine injection, electrocautery, endoscopic clips.
When is angiography/embolization used in Lower GI Bleeding?
For persistent bleeding not controlled by colonoscopy.
What are the indications for surgery in Lower GI Bleeding?
Recurrent hemorrhage, complicated diverticulitis, or uncontrolled bleeding.
How are hemorrhoids treated in Lower GI Bleeding?
Dietary fiber, sitz baths, surgical intervention if needed.
What is the treatment approach for fistulas causing Lower GI Bleeding?
Surgical management.
What are the stabilization measures for Upper GI Bleeding?
IV fluids (500-1000 mL boluses), blood transfusion (hemoglobin <9 g/dL high-risk, <7 g/dL low-risk), FFP (INR >1.8), platelets (thrombocytopenia).
What is the PPI regimen for Upper GI Bleeding?
IV PPI, 80 mg bolus, then 40 mg every 12 hours.
Which medication is given before endoscopy in Upper GI Bleeding?
Erythromycin, 250 mg IV.
What medications are used for variceal bleeding in Upper GI Bleeding?
Somatostatin or octreotide.
What antibiotics are used for Upper GI Bleeding in cirrhosis patients?
Ceftriaxone or similar antibiotics.
What endoscopic treatments are used for Upper GI Bleeding?
Band ligation or sclerotherapy (varices), injection/thermal therapy (ulcers, Forrest Ia-IIb).
When is balloon tamponade used in Upper GI Bleeding?
Temporizing measure for uncontrolled variceal bleeding.
What are the options for refractory variceal bleeding in Upper GI Bleeding?
Transjugular Intrahepatic Portosystemic Shunt (TIPS) or surgery.
How is re-bleeding prevented in Upper GI Bleeding?
Beta-blockers, variceal band ligation, or TIPS.

GIT Motility

What are the treatments for achalasia?
Pharmacologic: Nitrates, Ca++ blocker (nifedipine, muscle relaxant); Endoscopic: Pneumatic dilatation (balloon), botulinum toxin injection; Surgical: Heller myotomy + Nissen, Toupet, or Dor fundoplication, Peroral endoscopic myotomy (POEM)
What are the treatments for gastroparesis?
Small frequent meals, avoid indigestible fibers, replace some calories from solid to calorically dense liquids; Prokinetic treatment (high doses: dopredone, motelium, prempram); Enteral feeding in resistant cases: Jejunostomy tube
What are the treatments for Ogilvie’s syndrome?
Conservative management, colonic decompression tube, cecostomy, right colectomy

Inflammatory Bowel Disease

What are the treatments for ulcerative colitis (UC)?
Remission: Aminosalicylates (mesalamine, sulfasalazine), corticosteroids (prednisone), methotrexate, antibiotics (more in Crohn’s), immunomodulators (azathioprine, mercaptopurine), biological treatment (TNF inhibitors: infliximab, adalimumab; anti-α4β7 integrin: vedolizumab); Active proctitis: 1 gm mesalazine suppository ± oral 5-ASA (1 gm/6h); Acute left-sided or pancolitis (mild/moderate): Oral and topical 5-ASA ± prednisolone (40 mg daily, tapered 5 mg/week, stopped after 8 weeks), Ca and Vit D with glucocorticoids; Glucocorticoids not for maintenance; Immunosuppressive (thiopurine); Severe UC: IV fluids for dehydration, IV glucocorticoid bolus, topical and oral 5-ASA, biological treatment; Surgical: Proctocolectomy (implied for severe/refractory cases); Maintaining remission: Aminosalicylates (mesalamine, sulfasalazine), biological treatment for moderate/severe UC intolerant/non-responsive to immunosuppression
What are the treatments for Crohn’s disease (CD)?
Glucocorticoids: Prednisolone (40 mg daily, reducing 5 mg/week over 8 weeks), budesonide (first choice for ileal disease, 90% first-pass metabolism, low systemic toxicity); Calcium and vitamin D supplements; Anti-TNF agents (infliximab, adalimumab); Combination therapy (anti-TNF + azathioprine); Elemental diet, TPN; Surgical: Resection of affected segments (implied for complications)

Metabolic Liver Diseases

What are the treatments for non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH)?
Lifestyle interventions: Dietary changes, physical exercise to promote weight loss and improve insulin sensitivity (7-10% weight loss target)
What are the treatments for alcoholic fatty liver disease (AFLD)?
Cessation of alcohol, nutrition, corticosteroids, pentoxifylline, liver transplantation
What are the treatments for hereditary hemochromatosis?
Venesection of 500 mL twice weekly for up to 2 years, deferoxamine (iron chelating agent), liver transplantation
What are the treatments for Wilson’s disease?
Penicillamine, trientine (syprine), zinc acetate (galzin), liver transplantation
What are the treatments for Budd-Chiari syndrome?
Treat cause, TIPS, anticoagulation; Chronic/fulminant: Liver transplantation + lifelong anticoagulation

HCC

What are the treatments for hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)?
Surgical: Resection, liver transplantation; Ablative therapies: Radiofrequency, percutaneous ethanol injection (PEI), microwave ablation (MWA), cryoablation; Transcatheter arterial chemoembolization (TACE); Systemic chemotherapy; Palliative treatment

Ascites

What are the treatments for ascites?
Spironolactone + furosemide, paracentesis
What are the treatments for spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP)?
Cefotaxime, norfloxacin
