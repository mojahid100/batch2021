GEP all surgery treatments Flashcards

Esophagus

What are the treatments for achalasia?
Pharmacologic: Nitrates, Ca++ blocker (nifedipine, muscle relaxant); Endoscopic: Pneumatic dilatation (balloon), botulinum toxin injection; Surgical: Heller myotomy +(antireflux operation) Nissen, Toupet, or Dor fundoplication, Peroral endoscopic myotomy (POEM)
What are the treatments for GERD?
Lifestyle modification; H2 blockers (ranitidine, famotidine); Proton pump inhibitors (pantoprazole, esomeprazole); Potassium-competitive acid blockers (vonoprazan); Surgical: Esophageal fundoplication
What are the treatments for cervical esophageal perforation?
NPO, total parenteral nutrition (TPN), drainage, broad-spectrum antibiotics (e.g., imipenem); Surgical: Repair if early
What are the treatments for thoracic esophageal perforation?
NPO, TPN; Surgical: Repair if early, drainage or late case surgical repair is very difficult and failure is high so oesophagectomy and stomach pull up + chest drainge
What are the treatments for corrosive esophageal injury?
Ensure patent airway; Chest and abdomen X-ray to detect perforation; Avoid emetics; Pain control; Antibiotics + corticosteroids; NPO; Endoscopy after 24-48 hrs: if mild lesion, start oral feeding, follow-up gastrographin swallow after 3 weeks; Severe cases: Jejunal-gastric tube (JGT) for feeding under endoscopic guidance, gastrographin swallow after 3 weeks, trials of dilatation (gradual 3-4 sessions); if dilatation fails: Surgical: Esophageal replacement (colon bypass or stomach pull-up)
What are the treatments for Zenker’s diverticulum?
Endoscopic: Diverticulo-oesophagostomy with linear cutting stapler; Surgical: Diverticulopexy and/or myotomy of the cricopharyngeus
What are the treatments for corkscrew esophagus?
Surgical: Myotomy from aortic arch to cardia
What are the treatments for sliding hiatus hernia?
Lifestyle changes; Surgical (if lifestyle fails): Cruroplasty + anti-reflux mechanism (Nissen 360°, Toupet 270°, or Dor 180°)
What are the treatments for paraesophageal hiatus hernia?
Surgical: Repair
What are the treatments for esophageal cancer?
Radical resection Lower third Left thoracoabdominal resection (esophagus + part of stomach, esophagogastric anastomosis in chest); Middle third: Ivor Lewis (2 incisions: midline for stomach mobilization, right 5th intercostal space for esophageal excision to cardia, anastomosis in chest); McKeown (3 incisions: as Ivor Lewis + cervical incision, anastomosis in neck); Blunt transhiatal total esophagectomy + anastomosis in neck; Thoracoscopic-laparoscopic approach + cervical anastomosis; Esophageal replacement; Palliative treatment

Stomach & Duodenum

What are the treatments for gastroparesis?
Small frequent meals, avoid indigestible fibers, replace some calories from solid to calorically dense liquids; Prokinetic treatment (high doses: dopredone, motelium, prempram); Enteral feeding in resistant cases: Jejunostomy tube
What are the treatments for acute gastric dilatation?
Nasogastric tube (NGT) with suction, fluid and electrolyte replacement

Benign Disease of Intestine

What are the treatments for irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)?
Dietary measures: Fiber supplementation, excess water, caffeine avoidance; Probiotics (Bifidobacterium infantis); IBS-C: Linaclotide; IBS-D: Antimicrobial (rifaximin); SSRI & tricyclic antidepressants
What are the treatments for Meckel’s diverticulum?
Surgical: Resection of the ileum and closure transversely or segmental resection
What are the treatments for intestinal tuberculosis (TB)?
Anti-TB drugs + sanatorial treatment; Surgical (for perforation, stricture, bleeding): Resection and anastomosis; For M. bovis: Right hemicolectomy
What are the treatments for small intestine fistula?
Conservative: Nutritional support by IV fluids, control of sepsis, sump suction for drainage of intra-abdominal abscess, nasogastric tube for ileus to reduce fistula output, somatostatin to inhibit intestinal secretion and motility, systemic antibiotics until sepsis controlled, skin stoma care; Spontaneous closure in 70-80% of cases, mortality 6-10%; Surgical (if conservative fails): Resection of fistulous segment (implied)
What are the treatments for ulcerative colitis (UC)?
Remission: Aminosalicylates (mesalamine, sulfasalazine), corticosteroids (prednisone), methotrexate, antibiotics (more in Crohn’s), immunomodulators (azathioprine, mercaptopurine), biological treatment (TNF inhibitors: infliximab, adalimumab; anti-α4β7 integrin: vedolizumab); Active proctitis: 1 gm mesalazine suppository ± oral 5-ASA (1 gm/6h); Acute left-sided or pancolitis (mild/moderate): Oral and topical 5-ASA ± prednisolone (40 mg daily, tapered 5 mg/week, stopped after 8 weeks), Ca and Vit D with glucocorticoids; Glucocorticoids not for maintenance; Immunosuppressive (thiopurine); Severe UC: IV fluids for dehydration, IV glucocorticoid bolus, topical and oral 5-ASA, biological treatment; Surgical: Proctocolectomy (implied for severe/refractory cases); Maintaining remission: Aminosalicylates (mesalamine, sulfasalazine), biological treatment for moderate/severe UC intolerant/non-responsive to immunosuppression
What are the treatments for Crohn’s disease (CD)?
Glucocorticoids: Prednisolone (40 mg daily, reducing 5 mg/week over 8 weeks), budesonide (first choice for ileal disease, 90% first-pass metabolism, low systemic toxicity); Calcium and vitamin D supplements; Anti-TNF agents (infliximab, adalimumab); Combination therapy (anti-TNF + azathioprine); Elemental diet, TPN; Surgical: Resection of affected segments (implied for complications)
What are the treatments for familial polyposis coli (FPC)?
Surgical: Panproctocolectomy with permanent ileostomy

Disease of Peritoneum & Mesentery

What are the treatments for spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP)?
Cefotaxime, norfloxacin
What are the treatments for ascites?
Spironolactone + furosemide, paracentesis

Liver

What are the treatments for non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH)?
Lifestyle interventions: Dietary changes, physical exercise to promote weight loss and improve insulin sensitivity (7-10% weight loss target)
What are the treatments for alcoholic fatty liver disease (AFLD)?
Cessation of alcohol, nutrition, corticosteroids, pentoxifylline, liver transplantation
What are the treatments for hereditary hemochromatosis?
Venesection of 500 mL twice weekly for up to 2 years, deferoxamine (iron chelating agent), liver transplantation
What are the treatments for Wilson’s disease?
Penicillamine, trientine (syprine), zinc acetate (galzin), liver transplantation
What are the treatments for hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC)?
Surgical: Resection, liver transplantation; Ablative therapies: Radiofrequency, percutaneous ethanol injection (PEI), microwave ablation (MWA), cryoablation; Transcatheter arterial chemoembolization (TACE); Systemic chemotherapy; Palliative treatment
What are the treatments for Budd-Chiari syndrome?
Treat cause, TIPS, anticoagulation; Chronic/fulminant: Liver transplantation + lifelong anticoagulation

Intestinal Obstruction

What are the treatments for Ogilvie’s syndrome?
Conservative management, colonic decompression tube, cecostomy, right colectomy
What are the treatments for intussusception?
Bypass + lateral anastomosis; Prevention of recurrence: Hyaluronidase, steroids, fibrinolysin, heparin, dextran
What are the treatments for sigmoid volvulus?
Preliminary resuscitation; Endoscopic decompression (early cases); Surgical (if decompression fails, complications like gangrene/perforation, or elective after successful decompression): Exploration and untwist; if viable, colopexy; if non-viable, resection; elective resection even if viable (recurrence >50%)
What are the treatments for mesenteric vascular occlusion?
Resuscitation, pre-op preparation; Surgical (if peritonitis): Urgent exploration (revascularization if viable, resection if gangrenous); Treat cause (AF, atherosclerosis)
What are the treatments for constipation?
High fiber diet, increase fluid intake, laxatives, prokinetics (anti-H4: tegaserod, prucalopride), new drugs (lubiprostone, linaclotide, plecanatide); Surgical: Colectomy, ileorectostomy (hypomotility); Biofeedback (pelvic floor dysfunction)

Spleen

Malignant Disease of Intestine

What are the treatments for caecal tumours?
Surgical: Right hemicolectomy (remove peritoneum, terminal 10 inches of ileum, caecum, ascending colon, hepatic flexure, right half of transverse colon) + ileotransverse anastomosis
What are the treatments for tumours of the ascending colon or hepatic flexure?
Surgical: Extended right hemicolectomy (middle colic vessel divided, resection to junction of right two-thirds and left third of transverse colon) + ileocolic anastomosis
What are the treatments for transverse colon tumours?
Surgical: Transverse colectomy (hepatic and splenic flexures, middle colic vessels, transverse mesocolon, omentum)
What are the treatments for left colon tumours?
Surgical: Left hemicolectomy (extends to rectum, inferior mesenteric vessels divided)
What are the treatments for sigmoid colon tumours?
Surgical: Sigmoid colectomy (sigmoid vessels divided)
What are the treatments for upper 1/3 rectal cancer?
Surgical: Anterior restorative resection
What are the treatments for middle rectal cancer?
Neoadjuvant chemotherapy + surgical: Anterior restorative resection
What are the treatments for lower rectal cancer?
Neoadjuvant chemotherapy + surgical: Abdominoperineal resection + terminal colostomy

Acute Abdomen

What are the treatments for acute pancreatitis?
Mainly conservative: Resuscitation, monitoring, no drugs of value, limited indications for surgery (e.g., necrosectomy); Severe cases: ICU admission, pain relief, fluid replacement, pancreatic and bowel rest, respiratory support, resistance of infection; Surgical: As needed for complications

Appendix

Pancreas

What are the treatments for chronic pancreatitis?
Conservative: Pancreatic extract, H2 receptor antagonist, manage diabetes (diet, insulin), pain management (analgesics, stop alcohol); Surgical: Distal pancreatectomy (if inflammation limited to tail), Whipple operation (if head involved)
What are the treatments for pseudopancreatic cyst?
Percutaneous drainage (US/CT-guided, less successful if fistula to duct); Endoscopic drainage with pigtail catheter (transmural); Surgical: Cystogastrostomy or cystojejunostomy (biopsy cyst wall to exclude cystadenocarcinoma); Occasionally associated with pancreatic abscess or fistula
What are the treatments for insulinoma?
Surgical: Enucleation, distal pancreatectomy, pancreaticoduodenectomy
What are the treatments for gastrinoma?
If detectable: In head: Whipple’s operation (pancreaticoduodenectomy); In body/tail: Distal pancreatectomy; If undetectable (small, avascular): Blind body and tail pancreatectomy, total gastrectomy; Omeprazole in large doses for 6-12 months
What are the treatments for pancreatic cancer?
Mannitol (if bilirubin >15 mg/dl); Palliative treatment for unresectable cases

Cholangiocarcinoma

What are the treatments for cholangiocarcinoma?
Intent to cure: Surgical: Resection, liver transplantation; Adjuvant/Neoadjuvant: Chemotherapy, radiotherapy, stereotactic radiotherapy; Intent to palliate: Biliary stenting, photodynamic therapy (PDT)

Anal Canal

What are the treatments for pilonidal disease?
Abscess: Surgical: Drainage, leave open; Sinus: Surgical: Opening of cavity with side tracks and phenol cauterization (left open), or excision of cavity with side tracks and suture closure
What are the treatments for anal fissure?
Acute: High fiber diet, laxatives, warm water baths, local anesthetic, GTN ointment (glyceryl trinitrate for internal sphincter relaxation); Surgical: Lateral sphincterotomy at 4 and 9 o’clock; Chronic: Surgical: Lateral sphincterotomy; Botox injection
What are the treatments for haemorrhoids?
1st/2nd degree: Conservative (high fiber diet, laxatives, avoid straining, suppository), injection sclerotherapy (phenol almond oil for fibrosis), rubber band ligation, photocoagulation (up to 100°C, coagulative); 3rd/4th degree: Surgical: Haemorrhoidectomy; Secondary: Treat cause, conservative
What are the treatments for anorectal abscess?
Surgical: Incision and drainage, look for internal fistula
What are the treatments for anal fistula?
Surgical: Fistulotomy (open and curette tract); Intersphincteric fistula: Divide part of internal sphincter (continence preserved); Transsphincteric fistula: Divide part of internal and superficial external sphincter (temporary continence disturbance)
What are the treatments for faecal incontinence?
Conservative: Stool thickening (bran), biofeedback (pelvic floor exercises); Surgical: Sphincter repair (sphincter damage), rectopexy (complete rectal prolapse), postanal repair (idiopathic incontinence), gracilis muscle transposition (severe sphincter damage)
What are the treatments for rectal prolapse?
Children: Spontaneous resolution, avoid constipation/straining, manual reduction + buttocks strapping, submucous injection of sclerosing material (ethanolamine oleate at 6, 12, 3, 9 for fibrosis); Adults: Partial: Submucous injection of sclerosing material, mucosal excision; Complete: Surgical: Rectopexy, rectopexy + sigmoidectomy, perineal proctectomy, Delorme’s, narrowing of anus
